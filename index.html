üìä THE EVIDENCE

Record-Breaking Credit Card Debt Crisis - A North American Epidemic

United States: Americans now carry $1.21 trillion in credit card debt ($3,633 per person), with balances rising $27 billion in just the second quarter of 2025. Nearly half of all cardholders now rely on credit cards to cover basic living expenses.

Canada: Canadians carry $124 billion in credit card debt ($3,179 per person), remarkably similar to their southern neighbors. Credit card balances grew 9.2% year-over-year, continuing 31 months of consecutive growth. The average Canadian credit card balance hit $4,562, the highest since 2007.

THE SHOCKING REVELATION: Despite different currencies, economies, and populations, Canadians and Americans carry almost identical per-capita credit card debt. This proves the psychological manipulation techniques work universally - banks are using the same playbook on both sides of the border.

Predatory Interest Rates - Legal Loan Sharking

United States: Major banks charge interest rates averaging over 20%, with some reaching 30% - rates that would make loan sharks blush. Research shows large banks charge significantly higher rates than smaller institutions.

Canada's Historic Admission: Until January 1, 2025, predatory lenders in Canada could legally charge up to 47.9% interest on loans - nearly 50%! The federal government finally stepped in, reducing the criminal interest rate to 35% and acknowledging that "predatory lenders can take advantage of the most vulnerable people in our communities".

Brain Manipulation Science - Identical Effects Across Borders

MIT neuroscience research reveals that credit cards activate the brain's reward center - the same region exploited by cocaine and amphetamines. This psychological manipulation works identically on Canadian and American brains, explaining why the per-capita debt levels are so remarkably similar.

THE SMOKING GUN: In controlled experiments, people bid more than TWICE as much for the same items when paying with credit cards vs cash. The psychological cost of spending $1 on credit feels like only 50 cents - whether you're spending US dollars or Canadian loonies.

Targeting the Vulnerable - A Cross-Border Problem

United States: Credit card companies systematically target vulnerable populations including low-income consumers, students, and those with bad credit. "Fee-harvester" cards marketed to subprime borrowers often provide credit limits of $250 but only $72 in actual buying power after fees.

Canada: The rates of payday and high-interest lender usage is higher among vulnerable populations, including low-income households, single parents, and Indigenous Canadians. Until 2025, these lenders could legally charge 47.9% interest - higher than many illegal loan sharks!

The Human Cost - Families Destroyed on Both Sides of the Border

United States: 7.18% of credit card balances are now 90+ days delinquent, representing millions of families facing financial ruin. The rise coincides with 48% of cardholders using credit just to cover basic necessities.

Canada: While serious delinquency rates (0.93%) appear lower, Bank of Canada research shows carrying a balance significantly increases the likelihood of missing future debt payments. Non-mortgagors carrying balances for 7-12 months are seven times more likely to experience financial stress. Alarmingly, 74% of Canadians used credit cards for essential purchases - higher than the US rate.

CANADA'S TOTAL DEBT PICTURE: Canada's $2.5 trillion in total consumer debt includes mortgages, auto loans, and lines of credit - not just credit cards. When comparing apples to apples (credit cards only), the crisis is nearly identical per capita. The real story is that the same predatory techniques are destroying families on both sides of the world's longest undefended border.
